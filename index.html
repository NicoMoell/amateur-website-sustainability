Paper thin photovoltaic cells

The future of sustainable energy

A power source for everyone - and everywhere

Recently, at the Massachusetts Institue of Technology (MIT) an ultra useful new way on gaining power has been discovered! They are Paper-thin solar cells and may just be the future of sustainable energy.

Why, you might ask? Its simple: These photovoltaic cells are very slim, suprisingly durable and practical. They can in theory withstand weather and can be attached to almost any surface - and turn them into an energy source! Its not just that though - in power-per-kilogram, they even surpass regular solar panals at creating energy. This can be especially useful when...

  • ...you are outside...
  • ...you are in an emergency situation and need electricity...
  • ...you don't have batteries with you and want to power other devices like a radio...
  • ...or you just want to live sustainably at home and create your own electricity, without the worry of huge solar panels on your roof!

And thats not all! With the new printing techniques and resources used for these solar cells makes it possible for them to be mass produced with affordable costs!
